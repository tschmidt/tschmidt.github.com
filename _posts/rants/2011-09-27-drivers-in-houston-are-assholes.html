Driving in Houston has to be one of the most stressful things I do on a daily basis. It's almost as if people get behind the wheel of their car, inject themselves with 15ml of testosterone and become the most aggressive, un-aware people on the planet.

I've lived and traveled to some of the most populated cities and nowhere does driving compare to the white-knuckle nightmare that I experience in Houston. I am typically a pretty laid back person. But being on the roads in this city sets my blood pressure to levels that would horrify most physicians.

I get that we are in "the south", home of the outlaw mentality, but common sense should still exist. Street signs are ignored, speed limits are a joke and don't even bother with turn signals. The common approach to advancing down the road is to cut-off the person in the lane next to you...even if there is a mile of clear road in the lane you were just in before attempting to sheer off the bumper of the vehicle in the other lane.

Not in a turn lane? Don't worry about it. Just cut across 6 lanes of traffic to make a right turn. Coming to the end of your lane? Please, by all means wait till the very last possibly second and then cut into another lane with sadistic abandon for the safety of anyone else. I mean really? It's their fault for being in your way, right? WRONG, YOU STUPID SONOFABITCH.

Oh, and don't worry about waiting till you are out of your car and there is no chance of you killing someone with a 2 ton blunt object traveling at 80 miles an hour. Just take out your cell phone and text your friends, family, baby mamma, drug dealer, or boo. Why make a phone call? I mean, it's not like you should have your eyes on the road or anything. All you're doing is driving.

Here's a question; do you know what pedestrian is? Did you know that they should have the right-of-way? Well, not in Houston. If you are walking across the street, apparently you are open game for anyone if a vehicle because they will run you down. How dare you get in their way. Can't you see how busy they are driving 20 miles-per-hour over the speed limit, texting their boss, and shaving or putting on make-up on? The nerve of you to walk in a designated zone and expect them to stop for you.

If you drive aggressively and think you are getting to your destination faster I have news for you, you aren't. If anything you are the cause of the traffic jams. When you cut someone off you set off a chain reaction where 15 people behind you have to slam on their brakes bringing traffic to a grinding, screeching halt. And for what? So you can get a few feet further?

All I'm asking is that we try and pull our heads out of our asses and try something crazy; actually driving like we are supposed to. You will be amazed at how much smoother things will run.
